How America Was Founded

The founding of America is a pivotal moment in history, marked by a series of events that led to the birth of a new nation. The American Revolution, which began in 1775 and concluded in 1783, played a crucial role in establishing the United States as an independent country.

Tensions between the American colonies and Britain had been escalating for years due to issues such as taxation without representation and restrictions on trade. The conflict intensified with events like the Boston Tea Party and the Battles of Lexington and Concord.

On July 4, 1776, the Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence, drafted primarily by Thomas Jefferson. This declaration asserted the colonies' right to self-governance and outlined grievances against the British monarchy.

The Revolutionary War ensued, involving significant battles like the Battle of Bunker Hill and the Siege of Yorktown. The war concluded with the Treaty of Paris in 1783, which officially recognized the United States' independence from Britain.
